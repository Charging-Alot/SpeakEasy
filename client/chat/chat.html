Perplexity Over Time While Training

"Perplexity" is used to determine how 'correct' or 'incorrect' Marvin's response to a given statement is. The higher the perplexity, the more wrong the response.

Marvin is trained with the same data set and is expected to respond a certain way every time. As he sees the same inputs over and over, he gets better and better at giving the right responses. At first, he's way off. Eventually, he always in the same range of correctness.
